## Featured Strategic Project: S&P 500 Portfolio Optimization
Static investment models ignore market volatility and incur hidden costs. This project engineers a realistic institutional framework for S&P 500 portfolio management. It targets the top 15 constituents by market capitalization and applies dynamic walk-forward simulations. 

The system automates annual rebalancing and resolves dual-class share redundancies. Advanced optimization methods include Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio, and Hierarchical Risk Parity. To ensure statistical robustness, the model uses Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage for covariance estimation. Real-world constraints limit asset weights between 2% and 25%. A 10 basis point transaction fee accounts for turnover friction. This approach transforms raw market data into a rigorous strategy that prioritizes risk-adjusted returns and operational reality.
